1. Overview

DSPL manufactures 8 product lines of crane safety electronics — ACD, DRM-3400, DJ-1005, E-DASH, F-Dash, WWSI, MRT Systems, and TPS. These are built from 246 active Bills of Materials (BOMs) using BK*/BL* item codes across a catalogue of 6,502 items.

  • Manufacturing cycle: Sales Order → Production Plan → Work Order → Material Issue → Manufacture → Finished Goods
  • Production team sees Sales Orders (items and quantities only — no prices or financial data)
  • All manufacturing notifications are delivered via Telegram
  • Production lead: Vivek Kulkarni (production.dgoc@gmail.com)
Never cancel submitted BOMs — they are linked to Work Orders, Production Plans, and parent BOMs. Cancelling cascades and can corrupt data. See the BOM management section for the correct procedures.

3. Step 1: Receiving Sales Orders

Review incoming orders

Who: Production Team  |  When: On Sales Order submission
  1. You receive a Telegram notification when a Sales Order is submitted — it lists items, quantities, and delivery date (no prices)
  2. Open the Sales Order in ERPNext: Selling > Sales Order
  3. Check the following:
FieldWhat to look for
ItemsWhich products to manufacture (ACD, DRM, DJ, E-DASH, etc.)
QuantityHow many units of each product
Delivery DateWhen the customer expects delivery — use this to prioritize
CustomerCustomer name (for reference only)
  1. Prioritize production based on delivery dates — earliest first
  2. You can also view pending orders in the PWA at #/production
Sales Orders are read-only for production. Financial fields (rate, amount, total, taxes, payment terms) are hidden — you only see items and quantities.

  3. Click "Get Items from Sales Orders" — this auto-populates items from pending Sales Orders

6. Step 4: Material Issue (Stock Entry)

Transfer raw materials to WIP warehouse

Who: Stores / Production Team  |  When: Before starting manufacture
  1. Open the Work Order
  2. Click "Transfer Raw Materials" button — this creates a Stock Entry of type "Material Transfer for Manufacture"
  3. The system auto-populates items and quantities from the BOM
FieldWhat to enter
Source WarehouseWhere raw materials are stored (e.g., Stores - DSPL)
Target WarehouseWork In Progress warehouse (e.g., Work In Progress - DSPL)
ItemsAuto-populated from BOM — verify quantities
  1. Verify that all items are in stock — the system will warn if stock is insufficient
  2. Click Save, then Submit
Always use the "Transfer Raw Materials" button from the Work Order instead of creating a Stock Entry manually. This ensures proper linking and quantity tracking.

7. Step 5: Manufacture (Stock Entry)

Record finished goods production

Who: Production Team  |  When: After manufacturing is complete
  1. Open the Work Order
  2. Click "Finish" button — this creates a Stock Entry of type "Manufacture"
  3. The system auto-populates:
    • Source section: Raw materials consumed (from WIP warehouse)
    • Target section: Finished good produced (to FG warehouse)
FieldWhat to enter
Items (consumed)Auto-populated — raw materials consumed from WIP warehouse
Finished ItemAuto-populated — the product being manufactured
QtyNumber of units produced
Target WarehouseFinished Goods warehouse (e.g., Finished Goods - DSPL)
  1. Verify quantities match what was actually produced
  2. Click Save, then Submit
On submit, the production team receives a Telegram notification confirming the manufacture entry. The Work Order status automatically updates to "Completed" when all units are manufactured.

8. Step 6: Quality check

Inspect finished goods

Who: Quality Team  |  When: After manufacture, before dispatch

Quality inspection is currently a manual process. Each finished unit should be inspected before being cleared for dispatch.

  1. Verify product functionality against the product specification sheet
  2. Check all connections, displays, and sensor readings
  3. Run a load test where applicable (ACD, DRM, WWSI, MRT)
  4. Record any defects or deviations
  5. If a unit fails inspection, log the issue and route to rework

Future enhancements

  • Quality Inspection DocType: Can be added as a gate before finished goods acceptance
  • CAPA integration: Manufacturing defects will feed into the CAPA system (see Complaint & CAPA Guide)
  • Serial number tracking: Each unit will be assigned a serial number for traceability

9. BOM management

Managing Bills of Materials

Who: Production Head  |  When: As needed

DSPL has 246 active BOMs using BK*/BL* item codes migrated from Krisp. Follow these rules strictly:

NEVER cancel submitted BOMs — they are linked to Work Orders, Production Plans, and parent BOMs. Cancelling cascades and corrupts linked documents.
Fix typeProcedure
Wrong rates Fix the Item master's valuation_rate → open the BOM → click "Update Cost" (works on submitted BOMs, no cancel needed)
Bulk rate fix Fix Item prices → go to Manufacturing > BOM Update Tool → click "Update latest price in all BOMs"
Structural change (add/remove/swap items) Create a new BOM separately → use BOM Update Tool to replace old BOM with new BOM across all parents
Last resort amend Cancel → Amend (creates a copy with amended_from). Only if BOM Update Tool cannot handle the change

Rate source (rm_cost_as_per)

SettingWhere rates come from
Valuation Rate (default)Item master's valuation_rate field — this is what DSPL uses (0 Purchase Orders means no stock valuation)
Last Purchase RateRate from the most recent Purchase Invoice/Receipt
Price ListRate from the specified Price List
To fix rates across all BOMs: update each Item's valuation_rate in the Item master, then use BOM Update Tool to propagate. No BOM cancellations needed.

13. Tips for production team

Best practices

Who: All production staff
  • Always create Work Orders from Production Plan (not standalone) for full traceability back to Sales Orders
  • Set planned_start_date on every Work Order so overdue tracking works correctly
  • Use the "Transfer Raw Materials" button from the Work Order to auto-populate items from BOM — do not create Stock Entries manually
  • Use the "Finish" button from the Work Order to auto-populate the manufacture entry
  • Check the production dashboard daily for pending orders and overdue Work Orders
  • Never cancel submitted BOMs — use the BOM Update Tool instead (see BOM management)
  • Keep WIP and FG warehouses separate for accurate costing and stock tracking
  • Verify stock before material transfer — the system warns on insufficient stock but catching shortages early avoids production delays
If you encounter a BOM error or need to change BOM structure, contact the Production Head. Do not attempt to cancel or amend BOMs yourself.

How to view

  1. Log in to dgoc.logstop.com
