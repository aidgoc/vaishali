  3. Fill in the DCR form:

Always fill a DCR after every visit, even recovery and relationship-building visits. This data drives your pipeline analytics and target tracking.

4. Step 2: Lead management

Manage and enrich leads

Who: Sales executive / Sales manager  |  When: Ongoing

How leads are created

  • Auto from DCR: When lead_generated checkbox is ticked on a DCR, the system auto-creates a Lead linked back to that DCR
  • Manual: Go to CRM > Lead > New in the desk
  • Apollo import: Use the "Import from Apollo" dialog on the Lead list (search by email or name)
  • Bulk sync: Automatic sync from Apollo "Push to CRM" list every 30 minutes

Lead age indicator

The Lead form shows a color-coded age indicator:

ColorAgeAction
Green0 to 14 daysFresh — follow up actively
Orange15 to 30 daysGetting stale — prioritise contact
RedOver 30 daysAt risk — immediate action needed

Key fields

FieldPurpose
ZoneNorth / South / East / West / Central
Lead SourceHow the lead came in (DCR, Apollo, Website, Referral, etc.)
Lead OwnerSales executive responsible for this lead

Apollo enrichment

Click the "Enrich from Apollo" button on any Lead form to auto-fill:

  • Designation (job title)
  • Website
  • Industry
  • LinkedIn URL
  • Apollo ID (for tracking)

Convert to Customer

When a lead is qualified and ready to buy, click the "Convert to Customer" button on the Lead form. This creates a Customer record and links the lead history.

Bulk enrichment runs automatically every 30 minutes, processing 10 leads per batch. You don't need to enrich leads one by one.

6. Step 4: Quotation

Create and track quotations

Who: Sales executive / Sales manager  |  When: Customer requests pricing
  1. Create from Opportunity: click Make > Quotation on the Opportunity form
  2. Or create standalone: Selling > Quotation > New
  3. Add items with quantities and rates
  4. The system auto-sets valid_till to 30 days from the transaction date
  5. Submit the Quotation to send to the customer

Temperature tracking

Set the quotation_temperature field to track deal health:

TemperatureWhen to use
HotCustomer is actively discussing, likely to close within 2 weeks
WarmCustomer is interested but no urgency, may close within 1-2 months
ColdLow interest, long timeline, or no recent communication
LostCustomer has declined or chosen a competitor

Marking a quotation as lost

  1. Click the "Mark as Lost" button on the Quotation form
  2. Select the lost reason category:
CategoryWhen to use
PriceCustomer chose a cheaper alternative
TechnicalProduct did not meet technical requirements
BudgetCustomer's budget was not approved
OtherAny other reason (specify in remark)
  1. Optionally name the competitor
  2. Add a lost remark with details
The customer quotation count indicator on the form shows how many quotations have been sent to this customer historically. Use it to gauge engagement.
When a Quotation is submitted, the originating DCR's conversion_status is automatically set to "Quoted".

7. Step 5: Sales Order

Confirm the order

Who: Sales manager  |  When: Customer confirms the order
  1. Open the submitted Quotation
  2. Click Make > Sales Order
  3. Verify items, quantities, rates, and delivery date
  4. Submit the Sales Order

What happens on submit

  • The originating DCR's conversion_status is set to "Won"
  • Managers are notified via Telegram with the full financial details
  • Production team is notified with the item list and delivery date (no prices shown)
  • Production team gets read-only access to the Sales Order — 54 financial fields (rate, amount, total, taxes, payment terms) are hidden from them
Production team members (Manufacturing User / Manager roles) can see items and delivery dates but cannot see any pricing, totals, or tax information. This is by design.

8. Step 6: Delivery Note

Dispatch the goods

Who: Dispatch / Warehouse  |  When: Goods are ready to ship
  1. Open the submitted Sales Order
  2. Click Make > Delivery Note
  3. Verify items and quantities being shipped
  4. Add transporter details and tracking information
  5. Submit the Delivery Note

Managers are notified via Telegram when a Delivery Note is submitted. The dispatch tracker view in the PWA shows all dispatches and their status.

9. Step 7: Sales Invoice

Bill the customer

Who: Accounts  |  When: After delivery or as per payment terms
  1. Create from Delivery Note: click Make > Sales Invoice on the DN
  2. Or create from Sales Order directly for advance billing
  3. Verify all amounts, taxes, and due date
  4. Submit the Sales Invoice

Managers are notified via Telegram when a Sales Invoice is submitted. Outstanding amounts are tracked in the debtor dashboard.

The system tracks outstanding receivables automatically. Use the debtor dashboard in the PWA to monitor aging invoices and overdue payments.

10. Step 8: Payment Entry

Record payment received

Who: Accounts  |  When: Payment received from customer
  1. Open the Sales Invoice
  2. Click Make > Payment Entry
  3. Select payment type: Receive
  4. Enter the amount received and payment mode (bank transfer, cheque, etc.)
  5. Enter reference number and date
  6. Submit the Payment Entry
Only "Receive" type Payment Entries trigger the Telegram notification to managers. The message format is: "Payment received: ₹{amount} from {customer}".

15. Tips for the sales team

  • Fill a DCR after every visit — even recovery and relationship-building visits. Gaps in DCR data mean gaps in your pipeline analytics.
  • Always set next_action_date on every DCR. This powers the follow-up tracking system and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.
  • Update quotation temperature weekly. Hot/Warm/Cold status helps managers prioritise coaching and support.
  • Mark lost quotations with a reason and competitor name. This data drives competitive analysis and product decisions.
  • Check the pipeline view weekly for stale deals. Any opportunity with no activity for 14+ days needs attention.
  • Use Customer 360 before every visit — know the full history before you walk in.
  • Log phone and WhatsApp follow-ups as Sales Interactions (not just physical visits). Every touchpoint counts.
  • Review your My Targets dashboard monthly — know where you stand against the ₹10.09 Cr annual target.
  • Install the PWA on your phone — you can check in, create DCRs, and view your pipeline even with spotty connectivity.
  • Use Apollo enrichment before calling a new lead — knowing their designation and LinkedIn helps personalise the conversation.
