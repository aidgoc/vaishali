1. Overview

The finance cycle in ERPNext follows four stages: Sales Invoice (bill the customer) → Payment Entry (record what they paid) → Bank Reconciliation (match payments to bank statements) → Period Closing Voucher (close the month).

Each stage depends on submitted documents. A draft document is invisible to the accounting engine — it creates no General Ledger (GL) entry, does not appear in receivables or payables, and is excluded from every financial report.

DSPL currently has 1,017 draft Sales Invoices and 267 draft Payment Entries. Because these were never submitted, the system shows no GL entries for them, outstanding amounts are wrong, aging reports are meaningless, and bank reconciliation cannot work. Fixing this is the single highest-impact action for the finance function.

Key roles

RoleWhat they can do
Accounts UserCreate, submit, and amend Sales Invoices, Payment Entries, and Journal Entries
Accounts ManagerEverything above + Period Closing Voucher, bank reconciliation, report access, cost center management

2. Process flow

Delivery Note / Sales Order | v [SALES INVOICE] -- submit --> GL entry created (Debtors Dr, Revenue Cr) | Customer outstanding increases v [PAYMENT ENTRY] -- submit --> GL entry created (Bank/Cash Dr, Debtors Cr) | Customer outstanding decreases v [BANK RECONCILIATION] -----> Match PE reference_no with bank statement | Confirms money actually arrived v [PERIOD CLOSING VOUCHER] ---> Close the month Transfer P&L balance to Retained Earnings Prevents backdated entries
The entire chain breaks if documents stay in draft. A draft Sales Invoice means no receivable is recorded. A draft Payment Entry means the payment is invisible. Both must be submitted for accounting to work.

3. Sales Invoice

Create and submit a Sales Invoice

Who: Accounts User  |  Where: Accounts > Sales Invoice > New

A Sales Invoice should be created from a Sales Order or Delivery Note (recommended for audit trail). On submit, the system creates a GL entry that records the receivable and notifies managers.

Key fields

FieldWhat to enterExample
CustomerSelect from dropdownReliance Industries Ltd
ItemsProduct, qty, rate (auto-fills from SO/DN)ACD-100 x 2 @ ₹1,50,000
TaxesGST template (auto from item tax)GST 18%
Due DateWhen payment is expected2026-05-15
Payment TermsNet 30 / 50% Advance etc.Net 30

What happens on submit

  • GL entry: Debtors (Dr) → Revenue (Cr)
  • Customer outstanding amount increases
  • Managers notified via Telegram
  • Invoice appears in Accounts Receivable report

Overdue alerts

If a submitted Sales Invoice remains unpaid 7 days past its due date, the system sends a daily alert to the sales owner and accounts team until it is paid or written off.

Draft Sales Invoices do NOT create GL entries. They do not appear as receivables. The customer's outstanding balance stays at zero. This is why DSPL's current receivables data is unreliable — 1,017 invoices are sitting in draft.

7. Bank Reconciliation

Match payments with bank statements

Who: Accounts User  |  Where: Accounts > Bank Reconciliation
  1. Download bank statement from your bank portal (CSV or Excel format)
  2. Go to Accounts > Bank Reconciliation
  3. Select the Bank Account and date range
  4. Click Get Payment Entries — shows all submitted PEs in that period
  5. Upload the bank statement
  6. Match each bank statement line with a Payment Entry using the Reference No
  7. For unmatched entries: investigate and create missing Payment Entries or Journal Entries
  8. Click Reconcile to mark matched entries as cleared
This is why adding Reference No (UTR/cheque number) on every Payment Entry matters — the system can auto-match bank statement lines when reference numbers match.

8. Period Closing

Close a financial period

Who: Accounts Manager  |  Where: Accounts > Period Closing Voucher > New

A Period Closing Voucher closes a month or quarter. It transfers the net P&L balance (Revenue minus Expenses) to the Retained Earnings account and prevents any backdated entries into the closed period.

  1. Complete all items in the month-end close checklist first
  2. Go to Accounts > Period Closing Voucher > New
  3. Set Transaction Date (last day of the period, e.g. 2026-04-30)
  4. Set Posting Date
  5. Select Closing Fiscal Year (e.g. 2026-27)
  6. Select the Closing Account (Retained Earnings)
  7. Click Save, review the P&L transfer amount, then Submit
Do not submit a Period Closing Voucher until all Sales Invoices, Payment Entries, Purchase Invoices, and Journal Entries for that period have been submitted. Once closed, you cannot post transactions into that period without cancelling the closing voucher.

12. Fixing the draft problem

DSPL has 1,017 draft Sales Invoices and 267 draft Payment Entries that have never been submitted. Until these are resolved, all financial reports, outstanding amounts, and aging data are unreliable.

Step-by-step cleanup process

Who: Accounts Manager + Accounts User  |  Estimated time: 2–3 weeks
  1. Go to Accounts > Sales Invoice list
  2. Filter: Status = Draft, sort by Posting Date (oldest first)
  3. For each draft, decide:
    • Real invoice? → Check that all mandatory fields are filled (customer, items, taxes, due date). Fix any missing fields. Then click Submit.
    • Test / duplicate / invalid? → Click Menu > Delete.
    • Unsure? → Check if a submitted version already exists for the same customer + amount. If yes, delete the draft.
  4. Repeat for all 1,017 draft Sales Invoices
  5. Then move to Payment Entry list, filter Status = Draft
  6. Same process: review, submit valid ones, delete invalid ones
  7. After cleanup: run Accounts Receivable report — outstanding amounts are now accurate
  8. Run Trial Balance — revenue and receivable balances are now correct
Draft Sales Invoices with missing mandatory fields (customer, items, posting date, company) will throw a validation error on submit. Those fields must be filled in before the invoice can be submitted.
Work in batches of 50–100 per day. After each batch, spot-check the Accounts Receivable report to confirm the submitted invoices are now showing correct outstanding amounts.

13. Month-end close checklist

  • All Sales Invoices for the month submitted (zero drafts for the period)
  • All Payment Entries for the month submitted
  • All Purchase Invoices for the month submitted
  • Journal Entries for accruals and provisions posted and submitted
  • Bank reconciliation completed for all bank accounts
  • Review Accounts Receivable report — follow up on 30+ day overdue
  • Review Accounts Payable report — plan supplier payments for next month
  • Review Trial Balance — investigate any unusual account balances
  • Period Closing Voucher created and submitted
DSPL's fiscal year runs April to March. Period closing should happen by the 10th of the following month (e.g. April close by 10 May). This gives time to collect late invoices and receipts.

14. Tips

Best practices for the accounts team

  • Always create SI from DN/SO (not standalone) — maintains the full audit trail from order to delivery to invoice
  • Always add bank reference number on Payment Entries — UTR, cheque number, or transaction ID. Without it, bank reconciliation is manual guesswork
  • Submit documents on the same day they are created — never let drafts accumulate. A draft is invisible to accounting
  • Review the debtor dashboard weekly — focus on invoices that are 60+ days overdue. Escalate to sales for follow-up
  • Run bank reconciliation at least monthly — ideally weekly. Catching discrepancies early is much easier than at year-end
  • Use Payment Terms templates — set up standard terms (Net 30, 50% Advance + Balance on Delivery) so due dates calculate automatically
  • Never delete a submitted document — cancel it instead. Deleted documents leave no audit trail; cancelled ones preserve history
  • Check outstanding before dispatching — review customer outstanding in the debtor dashboard before approving new dispatches for customers with overdue balances
