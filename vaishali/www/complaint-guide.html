1. Overview

Every site complaint about DSPL products (ACD, DRM-3400, DJ-1005, E-DASH, F-Dash, WWSI, MRT, TPS) must be recorded, investigated, fixed, and closed with a root cause analysis. This ensures:

  • Customers get timely response and resolution
  • We find the root cause, not just a quick fix
  • Preventive actions stop the same problem from recurring
  • Management has visibility into complaint trends
Our products are safety-critical (crane overload protection, driver monitoring). P1 (Safety) complaints must be responded to within 24 hours.

6. Step 4: Fix and confirm

Apply the corrective action

Who: Service Engineer  |  When: During the visit
  1. Apply the fix:
    • Part replacement: Swap PCB / sensor / display from service kit
    • Recalibration: Recalibrate with reference weights
    • Firmware update: Flash latest firmware
    • Rewiring: Fix installation defects
  2. Test the system under load to confirm the fix works
  3. Get customer sign-off (signature or WhatsApp confirmation)
  4. In ERPNext:
    • Update Warranty Claim status to Work In Progress
    • Fill in Resolution Details
    • If a part was replaced: create a Stock Entry (Material Issue)
    • Note the old part serial number
Return the defective part to the factory for failure analysis (Step 5).

7. Step 5: Root cause analysis (RCA)

Find the root cause using 5-Why

Who: Production Head / R&D  |  When: Within 5 working days
  1. Examine the returned defective part
  2. In the Warranty Claim, expand the Root Cause Analysis section
  3. Select the Root Cause Category:
CategoryExamples
Design DefectCircuit design flaw, insufficient protection
Manufacturing DefectSoldering error, assembly mistake
Component QualityFailed capacitor, substandard PCB
Installation ErrorWrong wiring, incorrect mounting
Customer MisuseOverloading, water exposure, wrong power supply
EnvironmentalExtreme heat, corrosion, vibration damage
Supplier IssueDefective component batch from supplier
Firmware BugSoftware error causing incorrect readings
  1. Write the 5-Why analysis in the RCA Details field. Example:
WHY 1: Why did the load reading show 20% higher? → The load cell output was drifting over time. WHY 2: Why was the load cell output drifting? → Moisture had entered the load cell housing. WHY 3: Why did moisture enter the housing? → The O-ring seal had degraded. WHY 4: Why did the O-ring degrade? → The O-ring material was not rated for outdoor exposure. WHY 5: Why was a non-outdoor-rated O-ring used? → The BOM specified a generic O-ring without IP rating. ROOT CAUSE: BOM specification error — no IP-rated seal for outdoor units.

9. Step 7: Close and review

Close the complaint and verify effectiveness

Who: Service Coordinator (close) + Service Head (review)  |  When: After fix + 30 days

Closing the complaint

  1. Call customer to confirm equipment is running normally
  2. Set Warranty Claim status to Resolved
  3. Set the Resolution Date

Effectiveness review (30 days later)

  1. Open the CAPA record
  2. Check: has the same failure recurred on any unit?
  3. Check: were the preventive actions actually implemented?
  4. Fill in the Effectiveness Review section:
    • Review Date
    • Recurrence Found — check if yes
    • Reviewed By
    • Review Remarks
  5. Set CAPA Status:
    • Verified Effective — no recurrence, actions completed
    • Reopened — recurrence found (managers auto-notified)
  6. Click Amend to update the submitted CAPA

11. How to use the ERP system

Creating a Warranty Claim

  1. Log in to dgoc.logstop.com
  2. Search for "Warranty Claim" in the top search bar
  3. Click + Add Warranty Claim
  4. Fill in the fields (see Step 1 above)
  5. Click Save

Setting priority and SLA

  1. Open the Warranty Claim
  2. Scroll to DSPL Service Management section
  3. Select the Priority (P1/P2/P3/P4)
  4. Response Due Date and Resolution Due Date auto-calculate on save

Creating a CAPA

  1. Search for "CAPA" in the top search bar
  2. Click + Add CAPA
  3. Link it to the Warranty Claim (the CAPA auto-links back)
  4. Fill in all sections (RCA, Corrective, Preventive)
  5. Click Save, then Submit

Checking overdue complaints

  • Managers receive a daily Telegram alert at 9 AM for any complaints past their SLA
  • To check manually: go to Warranty Claim list, filter by Status = Open and sort by Complaint Date

13. Exceptions and edge cases

SituationWhat to do
Customer is in a remote location (500+ km)Remote diagnosis first via video call. Ship replacement unit for P1/P2. Schedule site visit on next trip.
Complaint during warranty periodStandard process. No charge. Track warranty expiry in the claim.
Complaint after warranty expiredInform customer of charges before dispatch. Create a Quotation for service + parts.
Same issue, 3rd timeAuto-escalate to MD. Consider product recall or design review.
Customer refuses field visitRemote diagnosis only. Document refusal. Do not close until confirmed resolved.
Third-party installation issueInvestigate installation quality. Feed back to installer training programme.
Bulk failure (5+ units, same issue)Batch recall process. Proactively contact all customers with affected serial numbers.
Customer threatens legal actionEscalate immediately to MD. Do not admit liability. Document everything.

14. Field engineer checklist

Before leaving for site

  • Warranty Claim number noted
  • Customer contact details confirmed
  • Product type and serial number known
  • Service kit stocked (spare PCBs, sensors, fuses, cables)
  • Laptop with latest firmware loaded
  • Calibration weights / reference tools packed
  • Camera / phone for documentation

At the site

  • Photograph the installation (wiring, mounting, environment)
  • Note firmware version displayed
  • Run diagnostic checks per product checklist (see Step 3)
  • Record all readings and error codes
  • Apply fix and test under load
  • Get customer sign-off (signature or WhatsApp message)
  • Collect defective part for RCA (label with WC number)

After returning

  • Update Warranty Claim in ERP (status, resolution details, first response date)
  • Attach site photos to the Warranty Claim
  • Hand over defective part to Production for RCA
  • If part replaced: create Stock Entry (Material Issue)
  • Update Maintenance Visit with work done
