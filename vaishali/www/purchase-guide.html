1. Overview

DSPL manufactures crane safety electronics — ACD, DRM-3400, DJ-1005, E-DASH, F-Dash, WWSI, MRT Systems, and TPS. Every one of these products is built from electronic components (PCBs, sensors, capacitors, resistors, connectors, relays) sourced from external suppliers. Right now, we have zero Purchase Orders in the system. This guide will help you start a formal purchasing workflow that gives DSPL real benefits:

  • Better pricing: PO history lets you negotiate with data — you can show a supplier exactly what you paid last time
  • Accurate stock: Purchase Receipts update warehouse quantities automatically — no more manual stock counts
  • Real BOM costs: Purchase prices flow into Item valuation rates, so your 246 active BOMs reflect actual costs instead of static guesses
  • Supplier accountability: Track delivery dates, overdue orders, and rejected items per supplier
  • Financial visibility: Know exactly what you owe suppliers and when payments are due

The good news: your ERPNext system already has 1,807 suppliers and 6,502 items loaded. You are ready to start transacting today.

The full purchase cycle has 6 steps: Material Request, Supplier Quotation, Purchase Order, Purchase Receipt, Purchase Invoice, and Payment Entry. Each step builds on the previous one, and ERPNext auto-fills details from the prior document so you do not have to re-enter data.

2. Process flow

Production / Department identifies need | v [1. MATERIAL REQUEST] Raise a request for items needed | v [2. RFQ] (Optional) Send to multiple suppliers, compare quotes | v [3. PURCHASE ORDER] Approve and send order to supplier | +--- schedule_date set | +--- overdue alert if not received v [4. PURCHASE RECEIPT] Receive items into warehouse (stock increases) | v [5. PURCHASE INVOICE] Record financial liability to supplier | +--- overdue alert if not paid v [6. PAYMENT ENTRY] Pay the supplier (liability cleared)
You do not have to follow every step every time. For urgent purchases, you can create a Purchase Order directly. But the full flow gives you the best traceability and approval control.

3. Step 1: Material Request

Raise a request for items

Who: Production team, any department head  |  Where: Stock > Material Request > New

A Material Request is a formal way to say "we need these items." It does not commit you to a purchase — it starts the approval process.

  1. Go to Stock > Material Request > New
  2. Select the Purpose (type of request):
PurposeWhen to useExample
PurchaseYou need to buy items from a supplierOrder 500 capacitors for next ACD batch
Material TransferMove items between warehousesTransfer PCBs from main store to production floor
Material IssueConsume items from stock (no purchase)Issue components for R&D prototype
ManufactureProduce items in-house from raw materialsManufacture a sub-assembly using BOM
  1. Add items to the Items table:
FieldWhat to enterExample
Item CodeSelect the item from the dropdownBKC01001
QtyHow many you need500
Required ByDate you need the items2026-05-01
WarehouseWhere the items should goStores - DSPL
  1. Set the Department (Production, R&D, etc.)
  2. Click Save, then Submit
On submit, managers are notified via Telegram. The procurement team can then review and create a Purchase Order from this request.

4. Step 2: Request for Quotation (optional)

Compare quotes from multiple suppliers

Who: Procurement  |  Where: Buying > Request for Quotation > New

Use this step for high-value purchases, new items, or when you want to compare pricing from multiple suppliers before committing.

  1. Go to Buying > Request for Quotation > New
  2. Add the items you need quotes for (or pull them from a Material Request using the Get Items From > Material Request button)
  3. In the Suppliers section, add 2-3 suppliers you want to get quotes from
  4. Click Save, then Submit
  5. Click Send Supplier Emails to email the RFQ to all listed suppliers
  6. When quotes come back, record them as Supplier Quotations (Buying > Supplier Quotation > New, linked to this RFQ)
  7. Compare quotes side-by-side and select the best one
  8. From the winning Supplier Quotation, click Make Purchase Order
You can skip this step for repeat purchases where you already have a preferred supplier and an agreed price. Go directly from Material Request to Purchase Order.

5. Step 3: Purchase Order

Place the order with a supplier

Who: Procurement  |  Where: Buying > Purchase Order > New

The Purchase Order is your commitment to buy. It tells the supplier what you want, how much, when, and at what price.

  1. Go to Buying > Purchase Order > New
  2. You can create a PO in three ways:
    • From Material Request: Open the submitted MR, click Make > Purchase Order (items auto-fill)
    • From Supplier Quotation: Open the SQ, click Make Purchase Order (items + rates auto-fill)
    • Standalone: Create a new PO and add items manually
  3. Fill in the key fields:
FieldWhat to enterWhy it matters
SupplierSelect from 1,807 existing suppliersLinks to supplier address, contact, payment terms
Items (Item Code, Qty, Rate)What you are orderingRate becomes the "Last Purchase Rate" for future reference
Schedule DateExpected delivery date for each itemEnables overdue alerts if supplier misses the date
Payment TermsWhen payment is due (e.g., Net 30)Auto-calculates due date on the Purchase Invoice
Taxes and ChargesGST template (IGST 18%, CGST+SGST, etc.)Correct tax calculation for Purchase Invoice
  1. Click Save, then Submit
  2. Optionally, click Print to generate a PDF and email it to the supplier
Always set the Schedule Date on every Purchase Order. Without it, the system cannot send you overdue delivery alerts.

6. Step 4: Purchase Receipt

Receive items into your warehouse

Who: Stores / Warehouse team  |  Where: Stock > Purchase Receipt > New

When items arrive from the supplier, create a Purchase Receipt. This is the moment stock increases in your warehouse.

  1. Open the submitted Purchase Order
  2. Click Make > Purchase Receipt (all items, quantities, and rates auto-fill)
  3. Verify the received quantities against the delivery challan:
FieldWhat to enterExample
Accepted QtyNumber of items that pass inspection480
Rejected QtyNumber of items that are defective20
Rejected WarehouseWhere rejected items goRejected - DSPL
Accepted WarehouseWhere good items are storedStores - DSPL
  1. Attach the supplier's delivery challan or packing slip
  2. Click Save, then Submit
After submitting, the stock ledger updates automatically. You can check updated quantities in Stock > Stock Balance or Stock > Stock Ledger.
Always create Purchase Receipts from the Purchase Order (not standalone). This maintains the link between what you ordered and what you received, enabling accurate delivery tracking and supplier performance measurement.

7. Step 5: Purchase Invoice

Record the financial liability

Who: Accounts team  |  Where: Accounts > Purchase Invoice > New

The Purchase Invoice records what you owe the supplier. It is based on the supplier's bill or tax invoice.

  1. Open the submitted Purchase Receipt (or Purchase Order)
  2. Click Make > Purchase Invoice (items, quantities, rates, and taxes auto-fill)
  3. Verify against the supplier's invoice:
FieldWhat to enterExample
Bill NoSupplier's invoice numberINV-2026-04-0892

Due DateWhen payment is due2026-05-20

  2. Click Save, then Submit
On submit, the accounts team is notified via Telegram. If the invoice is not paid by the due date, a daily alert fires at 9 AM to remind accounts.

8. Step 6: Payment Entry

Record payment to the supplier

The Payment Entry records the actual payment made to the supplier. It clears the liability created by the Purchase Invoice.

  1. Open the submitted Purchase Invoice
  2. Click Make > Payment Entry (amount, supplier, and references auto-fill)
  3. Verify the payment details:
FieldWhat to enterExample

On submit, managers are notified via Telegram. The outstanding amount on the Purchase Invoice drops to zero (or the partial amount paid). You can check all outstanding payables in Accounts > Accounts Payable.

11. Dashboards

Procurement dashboard

Where: Vaishali AI chat or Desk reports

The following views give you a real-time snapshot of your purchasing pipeline:

  • Pending Material Requests: Items requested but not yet ordered
  • Open Purchase Orders: Orders placed but not fully received
  • Pending Purchase Receipts: Items ordered but not yet delivered
  • Supplier Payables: Outstanding amounts owed to each supplier

  • Stock > Stock Balance — current warehouse quantities
  • Accounts > Accounts Payable — outstanding supplier balances with ageing

12. Tips for getting started

Your first week with formal purchasing

You are starting from zero Purchase Orders — that is perfectly fine. Here is how to begin.
  1. Start with your top 10 high-value components. Pick the 10 items you buy most often or spend the most on (PCBs, load cells, display modules, relay boards). Create POs for these first.
  2. Create POs for your next production batch. When the next batch of ACD or DRM-3400 goes into production, create a Material Request for all the BOM items, then convert it to Purchase Orders.
  3. Use "Make Purchase Receipt" from PO. When items arrive, open the PO and click Make > Purchase Receipt. All items, quantities, and rates auto-fill — no re-typing needed.
  4. Use "Make Payment Entry" from PI. When you pay a supplier, open the Purchase Invoice and click Make > Payment Entry. The amount and supplier details auto-fill.
  5. Set schedule_date on every PO. This is the expected delivery date. Without it, overdue alerts will not fire and you lose visibility into late deliveries.
  6. Review the procurement dashboard weekly. Check Accounts Payable and open POs every Monday to stay on top of pending deliveries and payments.
Each Purchase Order you create builds a price history for that supplier and item. After a few months, you will have real data to negotiate better rates, compare suppliers, and identify price trends.

13. Common mistakes to avoid

MistakeWhy it is a problemWhat to do instead
Receiving items without a PO No record of what was ordered, no price comparison, no delivery tracking Always create a Purchase Order first, even for small orders. Then use "Make Purchase Receipt" from the PO.
Not setting schedule_date on POs Overdue delivery alerts will not fire — late shipments go unnoticed Set the expected delivery date on every PO line item. Ask your supplier for a delivery commitment.
Paying a supplier without a Purchase Invoice The financial liability is not recorded — your payables report will be wrong Create a Purchase Invoice first (it records the liability), then create a Payment Entry from it (records the payment).
Creating standalone Purchase Receipts No link to the PO — you lose traceability between what was ordered and what was received Always create Purchase Receipts from the Purchase Order using "Make > Purchase Receipt".
Entering wrong rates on the PO The "Last Purchase Rate" for the item will be wrong, affecting future BOM cost calculations Double-check rates against the supplier quote or agreed price before submitting the PO.
Not recording rejected items Rejected items still show as received stock — your inventory count will be wrong Use the Rejected Qty field in Purchase Receipt. Rejected items go to a separate warehouse for returns.
Forgetting to attach supplier invoice Hard to reconcile during audits — no proof of the original bill Always attach a scan or photo of the supplier invoice to the Purchase Invoice in ERPNext.

14. How purchase fixes your BOM costs

From static guesses to real costs

This is one of the biggest benefits of formal purchasing for DSPL.

The current situation

Right now, DSPL has zero Purchase Orders. This means the Item valuation_rate for each of your 6,502 items is a manually entered static number. When a BOM calculates its cost, it uses this static rate — which may be months or years out of date.

What changes with Purchase Orders

Every time you submit a Purchase Order, ERPNext records the Last Purchase Rate for each item. Every time you submit a Purchase Receipt, ERPNext updates the item's Valuation Rate based on actual purchase prices (using the weighted average method).

This means your 246 active BOMs can now use real, current prices instead of guesses.

How to update BOM costs

  1. After a few Purchase Receipts, your items will have updated valuation rates
  2. Go to Manufacturing > BOM Update Tool
  3. Select "Update latest price in all BOMs"
  4. Click Submit — all 246 BOMs recalculate their costs from real purchase prices

You can also update a single BOM: open it and click Update Cost (works on submitted BOMs, no cancel needed).

Never cancel submitted BOMs to fix rates. Instead, update the Item's valuation_rate (which happens automatically via Purchase Receipts) and then use "Update Cost" on the BOM or the BOM Update Tool for bulk updates.
After 3 months of formal purchasing, you will have accurate product costs for all 8 product lines. This is essential for setting competitive prices, calculating margins, and making profitable quotations.
